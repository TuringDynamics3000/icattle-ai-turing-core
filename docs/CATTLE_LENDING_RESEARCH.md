## Key Findings

### Market Size
- **Total Australian agribusiness lending**: Over $140 billion as of September 2025 (80% increase since mid-2019)
- **NAB market share**: NAB lends more than $1 in $3 to Australian farmers (approximately 33% market share)
- **Banks dominate**: 93% of all rural business lending comes from banks

### Loan-to-Value Ratios (LVR)
- **Standard livestock LVR**: 70-80% of livestock value
- **Interpretation**: Farmers need to contribute 20-30% equity, with banks financing 70-80%
- **Rural property LVR**: 
  - Land up to 100 ha: 70-80% (case by case basis)
  - Land over 100 ha: Lower LVR, higher scrutiny
- **General banking standard**: LVR over 80% considered higher risk

### Interest Rates (2025)
- **Rabobank variable base rate**: Reduced by 0.25% in August 2025 and May 2025
- **RIC concessional farm loans**: 5.18% (held from February 2025)
- **Trend**: Interest rates declining in 2025 after increases in 2022-24

### Types of Livestock Loans

1. **Breeding Livestock Loans**
   - Longer-term loans (several years)
   - Livestock itself serves as collateral
   - Used to purchase breeding stock

2. **Feeder Livestock Loans**
   - Short-term loans
   - Cover acquisition and feed costs during growth period
   - Repayment expected from sale of animals

3. **Stocker Cattle Loans**
   - Specifically for cattle
   - Finance purchase for grazing over specific period
   - Include operating expenses for pasture

4. **Operating Loans/Lines of Credit**
   - Flexible, short-term
   - Cover day-to-day expenses (feed, veterinary, operational costs)
   - Help manage seasonal cash flow

### Loan Terms
- **Term loans**: 5-10 years for breeding stock and equipment
- **Operating loans**: Short-term with annual maturity, draw as needed
- **Repayment structure**: Aligned with production cycles and sale periods

### Collateral Requirements
- **Primary collateral**: The livestock being purchased/bred
- **Additional collateral**: May include equipment, land, accounts receivable
- **Valuation**: Lenders assess livestock value and farm assets
- **Security**: Entire herd may serve as security (e.g., cow-calf operations)

### Loan Application Requirements
- Detailed business plan
- Financial projections showing ability to repay
- Market analysis
- Operational strategy
- Clear understanding of production cycles
- Good working relationship with lender

### NAB Specific Services
- Specialist agribusiness relationship managers
- Trade and Working Capital specialists
- Flexible payment options for seasonal cash flow
- Full range of banking services
- Support for trading stock, feedlotting, backgrounding, breeding livestock

### Rabobank Specific Services
- Global agricultural lending expertise
- Specialized in agricultural finance
- Variable rate rural loans
- Risk management products

## Implications for iCattle Dashboard

### Realistic Financial Metrics
1. **LVR**: Use 70% as standard (conservative, bank-friendly)
2. **Interest rates**: 5.5-6.5% range for 2025
3. **Loan amounts**: Based on 70-80% of cattle valuation
4. **Equity cushion**: 20-30% (difference between 100% and LVR)

### Risk Assessment Factors
1. **Health status**: Critical for collateral value
2. **Market prices**: Affects both valuation and loan eligibility
3. **Diversification**: Concentration risk matters to lenders
4. **Compliance**: NLIS registration, animal welfare standards
5. **Production cycle**: Cash flow alignment with debt service

### Bank View Enhancements
- Show equity cushion (30% at 70% LVR)
- Display debt service coverage ratio
- Include collateral quality assessment
- Show compliance with lending standards
- Risk rating based on health, diversification, LVR

## Recommended Dashboard Updates

1. **Update LVR calculation**: Change from 70% to reflect realistic lending (70-80% range)
2. **Add interest rate assumptions**: Show assumed rate (5.5-6.5%)
3. **Calculate debt service coverage**: Annual income / annual debt payments
4. **Show equity position**: Total value - assumed loan amount
5. **Add loan type indicators**: Breeding vs. feeder vs. stocker
6. **Include collateral assessment**: Health status affects collateral value
7. **Production cycle alignment**: Show when cattle will be sold vs. loan maturity
